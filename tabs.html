Tyrannosaurus rex

The most famous dinosaur of all times. It's name means Tyrant lizard king. Probably the only dinosaur known by it's full name. It was hailed by the New York times as the ultimate warlord of the earth and another magazine called it the royal man-eater of the jungle. They gave T-Rex an upright stance but as time passed, it's posture became more horizontal. It's estimated running speed is around 12 mph. If it runs faster than 12 mph, it's legs will break due to the pressure on it. After all, it is a 5 ton predator. It's length is 40 feet and it was discovered by Barnum Brown in 1912. It lived 65 million years ago in the Late Cretaceous. Research suggests that it might have had feathers. The most complete and largest specimen of Tyrannosaurus was nicknamed Sue. Tyrannosaurus probably hunted Triceratops and some other dinos living in it's time. Tyrannosaurus was the dominant predator in it's area at the time. It is said to have the strongest bite in the dinosaur world. It was said to be able to eat bones as they have recently discovered coprolites Fossilized poop containing bones. At least, they think it was Tyrannosaurus dung. If you want to know more, read this Wikipedia article about Tyrannosaurus rex.

Carcharodontosaurus Saharicus

It's name means Shark toothed lizard. It is a part of a family of therapods called Carcharodontosaurs. It has 8in curved,serrated teeth in a 1.5 meter skull and it's length was 43 feet. It was a 6 ton predator and it was probably larger than T-rex. It's bite was weaker and it was less intelligent. Studies have shown that Tyrannosaurus's braincase Where the brain is stored is 150% bigger than Carcharodontosaurus's. Carcharodontosarus was still the killer of herbivores like Ouranosaurus and Paralititan.Carcharodontosarus looks like a predator with speed. It lived alongside Spinosaurus which is the largest predator. Scientists think that Spinosaurus ruled the water while Carcharodontosarus ruled the land. In-fighting was common among Carcharodontosarus as fossils show tooth marks left by other Carcharodontosarus for food and territory. Occasionally, fights between Carcharodontosarus and Spinosaurus would have broke out over food but the environment could support both animals. Carcharodontosarus lived around 90 million years ago. If you want to know more, check out this Wikipedia article about Carcharodontosarus

Spinosaurus Aeygptacus

It had 4 foot claws on arms strong enough to puncture steel. It is currently the largest carnivorous Meat-eating dinosaur ever known reaching lengths up to 60ft and weighing in at 9 tons. It also had a sail that is about 6ft tall. It had crocodilian jaws lined with conical jaws. Spinosaurus is now coming to the forefront as one of the mightiest beasts of the Mesozoic era. It belongs to a family of therapods called Spinosaurs. It is most likely a piscivorous Fish-eating as studies have shown that it spends more time in water. How could a beast that big support it self on fish? The fish living that live in the area that Spinosaurus lives are giants. Take the 8m long Onchopristis. It is a giant swordfish and the predator prey relationship between Spinosaurus and Onchopristis is confirmed because there is Onchopristis needles in Spinosaurus skulls and Spinosaurus teeth in Onchopristis bodies. Spinosaurus lived alongside Carcharodontosarus. Carcharodontosarus had more powerful jaws but it was smaller so both animals were mathched up even in a fight. Spinosaurus lived around 90 million ago. If you want to know more, check out this Wikipedia article about Spinosaurus.

Mapusaurus Roseae

Mapusaurus, a predator also larger than Tyrannosaurus. It belongs in a family of dinosaurs called Carcharodontosaurs. It lived in South America. It is a 5 ton predator around 42 feet long. Mapusaurus hunted the largest land animal of all times: Argentinosaurus. It hunted in packs. A fossil find showed 7 different Mapusaurus skletons jumbled in a mess on a hillside. They all died at the same time. This find proves (Hopefully) that Mapusaurus hunted in packs. Anyways, one Mapusaurus alone could not take on a 90 ton Argentinosaurus. It lived alongside Giganotosaurus. So there would have been fights. Dinosaurs like Mapusaurus, Carcharodontosarus, Argentinosaurus, Spinosaurus, Paralititan and Giganotosaurus represented the apex Peak of dinosaur development. After that point, things began to diminish in scale. Mapusaurus lived around 90 million years ago. If you want to know more, check out this Wikipedia article about Mapusaurus.

Diplodocus longus

Diplodocus, A 19 ton giant that is 90 ft or longer. It can reach lengths up to 120 ft and reach weights up to 25 tons. It's name meant Double-beamed. The 83 ft long replica that sits in London's Natural History Museum was given by King Edward VII in 1905. The first Diplodocus skleton was found by Benjamin Mudge and Samuel Wendell Williston in 1877. Diplodocus belongs to a group of sauropods called Diplodocids. The group includes Apatosaurus and other dinosaurs. A fossilized skin impression showed that it's back carried a row of spines A row of needles probably to cope with the stresses of mating. Diplodocus was slender compared to dinosaurs like Apatosaurus and Brachiosaurus but it would still have posed to be a challenge to predators such as Allosaurus. This giant lived around 150 million years ago in the Late Jurassic. If you want to know more, check out this Wikipedia article about Diplodocus.

Brachiosaurus Alithorax

Brachiosaurus, A 40 ton giant that can reach lengths up to 85 feet and heights of up to 40 ft. Brachiosaurus belongs to a group of sauropods called Brachiosaurs. It is estimated that Brachiosaurus eats up to 260 pounds in cycads, conifers, and gingko leaves everyday. Flowers haven't evolved yet. They will evolve later in the Cretaceous period. Brachiosaurus lived alongside Diplodocus and Apatosaurus. Brachiosaurus could not gain height by rearing up on it's hind legs because it's centre of gravity Center of mass was too fat forward and their limbs to weak to withstand the force of it's great bulk crashing on the ground. Brachiosaurus lived around 150 million years ago. If you want to know more, check out this Wikipedia article about Brachiosaurus.

Stegosaurus Armatus

Stegosaurus, an 4 ton beast that is around 30 feet long. It carries a row of plates on it's back and a Thagomizer Spikes on tail with 4 spikes. Stegosaurus had a very small brain in proportion to it's bosy size which suggests that it was the brighest of dinosaurs. It belongs to a group of Ornithischians One of the 2 dinosaur families called Stegosaurs. The Thagomizers were probably used for defense against predators like Allosaurus. The plates on it's back would have probably been there for sexual display. It's name means Roofed lizard. They thought that it was some sort of giant turtle at first but it turned out to be the Stegosaurus that we know today. If you want to know more, check out this Wikipedia article about Stegosaurus.

Triceratops, a 10 ton beast that is also 30 feet long. It has 2 long horns coming up from it's frills and a small nose horn. It belongs to a group of Ornithisician dinosaurs called Ceratopsians. Triceratops had a large frill that is common in Ceratopsians. Those horns come in handy when defending it self from Tyrannosaurus. It could have used it's horns to gore the attacker. It's frills would have been in use for sexual display. Triceratops was discovered by John Bell Hatcher in 1888. It was named Triceratops the next year by Othniel Charles Marsh. Fossilized skin impressions show that it might have sprouted bristles. There is no evidence that Triceratops herded as the largest groupings were found in groups of 3.
